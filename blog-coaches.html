For the Coaches

There's Enthusiasm - and then there's 'Enthusiasm'

Tips for Coaches/Parents of young players, January 22nd 2020

So your a coach of an under-10's team (and perhaps even a parent of 1-2 of the players). You've stepped since no-one else will, and a part of your conscious tells you that these kids deserve to have there weekly soccer. You might start to get feelings of ambition, of producing a great team, and even of producing the next Manchester United class of '92 (okay maybe that last part is a bit dramatic). The point being, loving the game is fantastic, and so we should if we're in it, as a player, as a coach, or as a fan. Some advice though - especially when coaching younger ages (6-12 years old), the best thing you can do for them is to keep them ENGAGED in the game. Any focus on results should be thrown aside and instead focus should be on creating a safe and fun environment for kids to develop - higher standards and winning can wait!
